About Publishing Extension Network

Learn about our mission, history, and commitment to strengthening local voices in Gilgit-Baltistan

Our History

PEN was founded in 1997 with the objective of strengthening local voices in the development and strives to empower the women and youth through mobilization, advocacy, information & knowledge management, capacity enhancement and research to promote sustainable human development.

Publishing Extension Network (PEN) is a registered non-governmental organization with the Government of Pakistan under the voluntary social welfare agencies ordinance 1961, bearing registration No.JC-16/(26)/1997 dated 11, Dec 1997. Since then this civil society organization has been working with the people of Gilgit–Baltistan.

Our Mission & Commitment

PEN is deeply committed to strengthening local voices and bridging communities by promoting development communication for sustainable human development.

  • Enhance Intercultural Communication
  • Support Local Communities in National and International Arenas
  • Seek Creative Solutions to Social Problems
  • Protect and Promote Cultural and Environmental Heritages
  • Strengthen Information and Communication Capacities

Our Journey

1997

PEN was founded and registered with the Government of Pakistan

1998

Organized first training workshop on "Mastering Basic Journalism"

1999

Conducted surveys on health and hygiene conditions in Ghizer and Gilgit

2004-2006

Implemented HIV/AIDS prevention project among border traders

2009

Participated in joint analysis workshop on conflict management in Bangkok

2013

Implemented Gender Equity Program with Aurat Foundation and USAID

2019-2020

Conducted research on Gender-Based Violence trends during COVID-19

2023

Implemented project on women political participation and democratic empowerment
